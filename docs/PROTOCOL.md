# Signal-to-Lane Protocol Specification

## Purpose

Signal-to-Lane Protocol exists to prevent platform chaos from corrupting long-term content architecture.

It allows a creator, researcher, operator, or campaign manager to use live platform demand without reorganizing their whole body of work around temporary algorithmic signals.

## Core Equation

Signal -> Campaign -> Test -> Evidence -> Lane

## Definitions

### Signal

A signal is a live or observed platform demand indicator.

Examples:

- TikTok Creator Search Insights term
- YouTube search gap
- recurring comment pattern
- high-performing post theme
- emerging public question
- platform-native content gap

### Campaign

A campaign is a temporary operating container created to test a signal.

It is not a permanent content lane.

### Lane

A lane is a durable content-library category organized by meaning, not platform demand.

Examples:

- theology
- civic reasoning
- personal reflection
- logic and debate
- devotional writing
- business systems
- music
- books

### Routing

Routing is the post-test decision that assigns a published or drafted asset to its permanent lane.

## Operating Boundary

A Signal-to-Lane campaign should not become a permanent lane unless repeated evidence proves the campaign subject is a durable content domain.

## Failure Modes

### Platform Capture

The creator lets platform demand determine identity.

### Lane Confusion

Temporary campaigns get mistaken for permanent archive structure.

### Rage Incentive Capture

Conflict becomes the content engine instead of clarity.

### Unlogged Learning

Posts are published but not evaluated, making the campaign non-reproducible.

## Completion Criteria

A campaign is complete when one of the following happens:

1. The signal is discarded as low-value.
2. The signal becomes a repeatable format.
3. The signal routes into an existing lane.
4. The signal proves the need for a new permanent lane.
5. The campaign is archived with findings.
